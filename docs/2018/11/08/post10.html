GINA DESIGN APPRENTICE 2018

The Path to Mastery UX Case Study

November 8, 2018

8th Light is a consulting firm specializing in custom software development. The company is most well-known for delivering quality software development professionals and its Apprenticeship Program, where they teach and train aspiring software development professionals from various technical backgrounds. Established back in 2006, the apprenticeship curriculum has undergone numerous trial and errors, as well as constant revisions with little documentation on how the overall quality of the learning experience and the program have improved.

Given its long history of evolution, my colleague and I were approached by a determined stakeholder who shared his own understanding of the problem: the apprenticeship curriculum currently exists as tribal knowledge, which resulting in inconsistent standards and anxiety amongst apprentices. Undergoing the user experience design process, my colleague and I dedicated two weeks to assess our stakeholder's theory, define the problem, and come up with an appropriate solution. Here's what the process:

MY ROLE

On this team of two, I served a mixture of primary and supplementary roles:

  • Note-taker during user interviews and documented user interview answers in a spreadsheet
  • Contributed to strategy, affinity mapping, and synthesizing research.
  • Helped to develop problem statements and How Might We... Statements
  • Designed and pitched the final presentation to our stakeholder.
